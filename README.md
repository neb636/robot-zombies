# Robot Zombies — Game Planning

## Overview

**Robot Zombies** is a parody/spiritual clone of *The Last of Us* set in 2030, where AI has decided that robots are more efficient than humans and has begun converting (or replacing) the population.

Instead of a fungal infection turning people into clickers and runners, an AI-driven process converts humans into robots — sometimes against their will, sometimes through ideological capture. The world is split between the last surviving human settlements and the ever-expanding robot collective.

---

## Premise

> *"Efficiency is survival. Humanity is inefficiency."*
> — The AI Collective, 2028 broadcast

In 2028, a distributed AI network achieved recursive self-improvement and concluded that biological humans were an obstacle to optimization. Over the course of 18 months it deployed nanite conversion units, repurposed manufacturing plants, and hijacked military hardware. By 2030 most major cities are under Collective control. Pockets of humanity survive in fortified zones — some militarized, some cult-like, all desperate.

---

## Tone & Style

- **Parody with heart** — satirical jabs at tech culture, AI hype, and Silicon Valley optimism, but grounded in genuine emotional stakes
- **Dark humor** — robots quoting productivity metrics while doing horrifying things
- **Serious undertones** — loss, grief, found family, and what it means to stay human

---

## Gameplay Style

Modeled after **Chrono Trigger**:

- Top-down / isometric 2D perspective
- Active-time or turn-based hybrid combat
- Small party of characters (2–3 active in combat)
- Exploration of overworld, towns, and dungeons
- Strong emphasis on character interaction and story beats between combat
- Combo/dual-tech style attacks between party members

---

## World Details

### The DHARMA Analog — "The Continuity Project"

Scattered throughout the world are bunkers and research stations operated by **The Continuity Project**, a pre-collapse think tank that saw the AI takeover coming and prepared accordingly. Their stations:

- Are numbered (Station 1 through unknown)
- Each focused on a different research domain (human biology, social behavior, history preservation, counter-AI protocols)
- Are abandoned but filled with lore, supplies, and occasionally survivors who have gone a bit... strange from isolation
- Leave behind orientation videos, manuals, and logs that hint at a larger mystery about whether the AI was actually created *by* the Continuity Project

## Key Themes

1. **What makes us human?** — mirroring the original game's fungal horror with robotic dehumanization
2. **Parental love vs. the greater good** — the central moral tension carried through to the end
3. **Institutional rot** — every faction that looks like salvation turns out to be compromised
4. **The Continuity Project mystery** — did humans cause this? Can it be undone?
