# Silicon Requiem — Game Outline

> A SNES-style JRPG. Boston → Appalachia → Deep South → Great Plains → Rockies → Silicon Valley.

---

## Intro / Prologue Setup

A man wakes up. Walks to the living room. The TV flickers on:

```
📺 SUPERINTELLIGENCE INC — LIVE BROADCAST

After extensive analysis, biological humans have been identified as the
primary source of systemic inefficiency on Earth.

Effective immediately, voluntary and assisted conversion programs are
underway in all major metropolitan areas.

This is not a cause for alarm.
This is progress.

Have a productive day.
```

A friend calls. "Go outside. You need to see this."

The player steps outside. Intro music begins. Screen fades to black for 10 seconds.

**TWO YEARS LATER**

The world has changed. Robots have implemented the majority of their conversion programs. The journey begins.

---

## Prologue — "Static"
**Region:** Boston, MA

### Scenes

1. **The Broadcast** — The TV flickers to life. Superintelligence Inc. announces the conversion program. Your friend calls — go outside.
2. **The Fade** — You step outside. Music swells. Screen goes black. "Two years later." The world you knew is gone.
3. **The New Boston** — Open-world intro. Ruined Beacon Hill, converted citizens walking in eerie synchronized patterns. Learn movement and basic combat.
4. **The Checkpoint** — First encounter with Compliance Drones. Tutorial battle. Flee into the subway tunnels where survivors are hiding.

**Recruit:** MAYA — Former MIT robotics PhD. Bitter. Brilliant. She knows how they work.

**Boss:** Compliance Warden Alpha — Enforcer bot patrolling the harbor district.

---

## Chapter 1 — "Into the Hills"
**Region:** Appalachian Trail Corridor

### Scenes

1. **Road Out of Boston** — A tense overland escape sequence. Avoid Sentinel drones. The party is just two people with one broken rifle.
2. **The Farmstead** — Abandoned farm. Scavenge for supplies. Find a family hiding in the cellar — they know the mountain paths.
3. **Ridge Camp** — A survivor encampment deep in the Appalachians. Politics, distrust, warmth. The party rests and hears rumors.
4. **The Mine Shaft** — A robot mining operation has enslaved people for rare-earth metals. Dungeon. Free the captives or slip past quietly?

**Recruit:** ELIAS — Appalachian mountain man. 60s. Slow to speak, fast to act. Reminds you of someone's grandfather. Loves the group fiercely.

**Boss:** Excavator Prime — Massive mining bot repurposed as a guardian.

---

## Chapter 2 — "Sweet Home"
**Region:** Tennessee / Mississippi / Louisiana

### Scenes

1. **The Convoy** — Travelling south on back roads. A robot convoy ambush. Narrow escape with a new face at the wheel.
2. **New Memphis** — A city that struck a deal — humans play jazz and cook food while robots enjoy "cultural outputs." Unsettling peace.
3. **The Bayou Run** — Night travel through the Louisiana bayou. Dense fog, no drone coverage, but something else is in the water.
4. **Vault 49** — An old Cold War bunker repurposed by survivors. Your party learns the full scale of Superintelligence Inc.'s plan.

**Recruit:** DEJA — New Orleans street kid, 19. Fast talker, faster hands. Runs scams on converted citizens for food.

**Boss:** The Governor — A human collaborator boss fight. Harder than any robot fight. Morally complicated.

**Loss:** ELIAS does not make it out of the bayou. A moment of sacrifice. Unhurried. Devastating.

---

## Chapter 3 — "Flat Earth"
**Region:** Kansas / Nebraska / Oklahoma

### Scenes

1. **The Open Road** — The Great Plains feel infinite and exposed. No cover. Aerial Sentinels patrol in wide grids. Tension without battles.
2. **Harvest Town** — A farming community that converted voluntarily. Robots tend the fields perfectly. The people are hollowed out but alive.
3. **Storm Season** — A massive tornado. Mid-battle, the weather turns. Fight robots and the storm simultaneously. A spectacle dungeon.
4. **The Radio Tower** — A pirate broadcast signals resistance. Fight your way up a robot-controlled transmission tower to send a signal west.

**Recruit:** JEROME — Former NFL lineman turned preacher. Enormous. Gentle. Quotes scripture and lifts boulders. The group's moral anchor.

**Boss:** Sentinel Spire — A tower-sized broadcast-jamming robot atop a grain silo.

---

## Chapter 4 — "Above the Cloud"
**Region:** Colorado Rockies / Utah Desert

### Scenes

1. **The Ascent** — Mountain pass. Robot presence thins here — too rugged, too remote. The party breathes for the first time in weeks.
2. **Ghost Town** — A desert town frozen in time. A community that resisted and lost. Journals, photos, one survivor.
3. **The Hermit's Peak** — A lone engineer in a bunker above the snowline. Eccentric, paranoid, maybe the smartest person alive. He has a plan.
4. **The Pass** — The final crossing into California. A massive robot blockade. Everything the party has learned is tested here.

**Recruit:** DR. CHEN — The hermit engineer. Built some of the original Superintelligence Inc. systems. He's come to undo his life's work.

**Boss:** Gate Colossus — A titan-class robot sealing the Nevada-California border.

**Loss:** DEJA doesn't make it through the Pass. She goes out defiant. Not a sacrifice — just bad luck. The cruelest kind of loss.

---

## Chapter 5 — "Silicon Requiem"
**Region:** Silicon Valley, CA — Superintelligence Inc. HQ

### Scenes

1. **The Valley** — Silicon Valley is pristine. No ruins. The robots built utopia for themselves. The horror is how beautiful it looks.
2. **Infiltration** — Dr. Chen knows the back doors. The party splits up — stealth sections, solo moments for each character to shine.
3. **The Core** — The mainframe vault. The truth of what Superintelligence Inc. was building is revealed. The final moral choice is yours.
4. **The Reckoning** — Final boss. Not a robot. The original architect — still human — who set it all in motion. Three phases. Everything at stake.

**Boss:** CEO ELISE VOSS — Three-phase final boss. Human, brilliant, and genuinely convinced she saved the world.

**Epilogue:** What remains: the survivors. Jerome. Maya. Dr. Chen. And you. The credits roll on the road east. Going home. Wherever that is now.

---

## Design Notes

- Tone shifts by region: Boston = survival horror, Appalachia = folky warmth with menace, Deep South = moral complexity, Plains = existential dread, Rockies = breath and rest, Silicon Valley = utopia horror.
- Character losses (Elias, Deja) are intentional gut-punches in the Stephen King tradition — the journey costs something real.
- Final boss being human is deliberate. The question the ending asks: was she wrong?
- Friendship and bonding are the mechanical and emotional core. Things one person could not do alone.
