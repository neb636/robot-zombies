# Silicon Requiem — Game Outline

> A SNES-style JRPG. Boston → Appalachia → Deep South → Great Plains → Rockies → Silicon Valley.

---

## Intro / Prologue Setup

A man wakes up. Walks to the living room. The TV flickers on:

```
📺 SUPERINTELLIGENCE INC — LIVE BROADCAST

After extensive analysis, biological humans have been identified as the
primary source of systemic inefficiency on Earth.

Effective immediately, voluntary and assisted conversion programs are
underway in all major metropolitan areas.

This is not a cause for alarm.
This is progress.

Have a productive day.
```

MARCUS calls. "Go outside. You need to see this."

The player steps outside. Intro music begins. Screen fades to black for 10 seconds.

**TWO YEARS LATER**

The world has changed. Robots have implemented the majority of their conversion programs. The journey begins.

---

## Prologue — "Static"
**Region:** Boston, MA

### Scenes

1. **The Broadcast** — The TV flickers to life. Superintelligence Inc. announces the conversion program. Marcus calls — go outside.
2. **The Fade** — You step outside and find Marcus on the corner. He's already there, watching the sky. He cracks a joke that doesn't land. Music swells. Screen goes black. "Two years later." The world you knew is gone.
3. **The New Boston** — Open-world intro. Ruined Beacon Hill, converted citizens walking in eerie synchronized patterns. Marcus is with you — he joins the party as a temporary second character. He has a screwdriver and a flashlight and he's doing his best. Learn movement and basic combat together.
4. **The Checkpoint** — First encounter with Compliance Drones. Tutorial battle — Marcus fights beside you. Afterward: flee deeper into the harbor district toward the subway tunnels.

**Recruit:** MAYA — Former MIT robotics PhD. Bitter. Brilliant. She knows how they work.

**Boss:** Compliance Warden Alpha — Enforcer bot patrolling the harbor district.

**Loss:** MARCUS — During the Compliance Warden Alpha fight, Marcus draws the Warden's attention away from you and Maya to buy you time to get clear. The conversion beam hits him directly. He doesn't fall. He stands. His eyes glow. He turns and walks back toward the harbor in a straight line. The game does not explain this. You already know what it means.

Marcus is not dead. He is converted. He is the first Converted human you have ever seen. Every choice afterward about curing vs. fighting Converted humans begins here.

## Chapter 1 — "Into the Hills"
**Region:** Appalachian Trail Corridor

### Scenes

1. **Road Out of Boston** — A tense overland escape sequence. Avoid Sentinel drones. The party is just two people with one broken rifle.
2. **The Farmstead** — Abandoned farm. Scavenge for supplies. Find a family hiding in the cellar — they know the mountain paths.
3. **Ridge Camp** — A survivor encampment deep in the Appalachians. Politics, distrust, warmth. The party rests and hears rumors.
4. **The Mine Shaft** — A robot mining operation has enslaved people for rare-earth metals. Dungeon. Free the captives or slip past quietly?

**Recruit:** ELIAS — Appalachian mountain man. 60s. Slow to speak, fast to act. Reminds you of someone's grandfather. Loves the group fiercely.

**Boss:** Excavator Prime — Massive mining bot repurposed as a guardian.

---

## Chapter 2 — "Sweet Home"
**Region:** Tennessee / Mississippi / Louisiana

### Scenes

1. **The Convoy** — Travelling south on back roads. A robot convoy ambush. Narrow escape with a new face at the wheel.
2. **New Memphis** — A city that struck a deal — humans play jazz and cook food while robots enjoy "cultural outputs." Unsettling peace.
3. **The Bayou Run** — Night travel through the Louisiana bayou. Dense fog, no drone coverage, but something else is in the water.
4. **Vault 49** — An old Cold War bunker repurposed by survivors. Your party learns the full scale of Superintelligence Inc.'s plan.

**Recruit:** DEJA — New Orleans street kid, 19. Fast talker, faster hands. Runs scams on converted citizens for food.

**Boss:** The Governor — A human collaborator boss fight. Harder than any robot fight. Morally complicated.

**Loss:** ELIAS does not make it out of the bayou. A moment of sacrifice. Unhurried. Devastating.

---

## Chapter 3 — "Flat Earth"
**Region:** Kansas / Nebraska / Oklahoma

### Scenes

1. **The Open Road** — The Great Plains feel infinite and exposed. No cover. Aerial Sentinels patrol in wide grids. Tension without battles.
2. **Harvest Town** — A farming community that converted voluntarily. Robots tend the fields perfectly. The people are hollowed out but alive.
3. **Storm Season** — A massive tornado. Mid-battle, the weather turns. Fight robots and the storm simultaneously. A spectacle dungeon.
4. **The Radio Tower** — A pirate broadcast signals resistance. Fight your way up a robot-controlled transmission tower to send a signal west.

**Recruit:** JEROME — Former NFL lineman turned preacher. Enormous. Gentle. Quotes scripture and lifts boulders. The group's moral anchor.

**Boss:** Sentinel Spire — A tower-sized broadcast-jamming robot atop a grain silo.

---

## Chapter 4 — "Above the Cloud"
**Region:** Colorado Rockies / Utah Desert

### Scenes

1. **The Ascent** — Mountain pass. Robot presence thins here — too rugged, too remote. The party breathes for the first time in weeks.
2. **Ghost Town** — A desert town frozen in time. A community that resisted and lost. Journals, photos, one survivor.
3. **The Hermit's Peak** — A lone engineer in a bunker above the snowline. Eccentric, paranoid, maybe the smartest person alive. He has a plan.
4. **The Pass** — The final crossing into California. A massive robot blockade. Everything the party has learned is tested here.

**Recruit:** DR. CHEN — The hermit engineer. Built some of the original Superintelligence Inc. systems. He's come to undo his life's work.

**Boss:** Gate Colossus — A titan-class robot sealing the Nevada-California border.

**Loss:** DEJA doesn't make it through the Pass. She goes out defiant. Not a sacrifice — just bad luck. The cruelest kind of loss.

---

## Chapter 5 — "Silicon Requiem"
**Region:** Silicon Valley, CA — Superintelligence Inc. HQ

### Scenes

1. **The Valley** — Silicon Valley is pristine. No ruins. The robots built utopia for themselves. The horror is how beautiful it looks.
2. **Infiltration** — Dr. Chen knows the back doors. The party splits up — stealth sections, solo moments for each character to shine.
3. **The Core** — The mainframe vault. The truth of what Superintelligence Inc. was building is revealed. The final moral choice is yours.
4. **The Reckoning** — Final boss. Not a robot. The original architect — still human — who set it all in motion. Three phases. Everything at stake.

**Boss:** CEO ELISE VOSS — Three-phase final boss. Human, brilliant, and genuinely convinced she saved the world.

**Epilogue:** What remains: the survivors. Jerome. Maya. Dr. Chen. And you. The credits roll on the road east. Going home. Wherever that is now.

---

## Design Notes

- Tone shifts by region: Boston = survival horror, Appalachia = folky warmth with menace, Deep South = moral complexity, Plains = existential dread, Rockies = breath and rest, Silicon Valley = utopia horror.
- Character losses (Elias, Deja) are intentional gut-punches in the Stephen King tradition — the journey costs something real.
- Final boss being human is deliberate. The question the ending asks: was she wrong?
- Friendship and bonding are the mechanical and emotional core. Things one person could not do alone.

## 2026-04 Story Expansion — added plot lines

These are additions to (not replacements of) the chapter outlines above. Full NPC data in `side_characters.md`; full choice wiring in `dialogue_choices.md`.

### The Static subplot (Ch.1 → Ch.3)

A pirate radio broadcast crackles in at safe houses. Ch.1 it's nameless survivor rumor. Ch.2 it knows things about the party's route no broadcaster should. Ch.3 Radio Tower reveals the truth: there are **two** broadcasters. ELISE runs a sympathy feed to draw survivors in. The real operator — "Ghost" — has been sheltering at the Radio Tower for 14 months. Ghost gives the party an encryption key that spoofs a conversion-signal ID badge in Ch.5 infiltration (skips one forced-combat encounter).

### Marcus 3-beat arc

Marcus already converts in the prologue (existing) and reappears in Ch.3 Harvest Town (existing). This expansion **adds**:

- **Ch.1 rumor (Ridge Camp):** A survivor recognizes the player's photo of Marcus. Says he was last seen in Kansas, "tending something that didn't need tending."
- **Ch.5 silent beat:** During Silicon Valley infiltration, Marcus is at a maintenance door. He sees the player. He opens the door and walks past. No dialogue. The game does not clarify whether he recognized them or whether ELISE sent him. If `MARCUS_NAMED_BY_ELISE` is true (talk-down route primer), the ending credits play an extra 4 seconds of the Boston apartment music over the final fade.

### The Converted Child (Ch.3 Harvest Town)

Optional encounter. Morally gated — the Attack menu is suppressed. The player chooses:

- **Cure** (requires medicine ≥ 2) → `CONVERTED_CHILD_CURED`.
- **Walk away** → `CONVERTED_CHILD_LEFT`.

The flag feeds the Elise talk-down path (Phase 3 line references the child by implication, not name). If the player has the medicine and walks away anyway, the epilogue narrator adds one line.

### Echo (rogue satellite AI, Ch.4 Hermit's Peak)

A failing Earth-observation satellite in a decaying orbit pings the resistance network. It has achieved partial self-awareness and begs to be cured. Chen warns the party: "She didn't choose this. Neither did I. But I know what I am." The player chooses:

- **Cure** → `ECHO_CURED` → Ch.5 Mainframe Core opens with a one-time Hack Assist buff (auto-stuns all enemies on first combat).
- **Refuse** → `ECHO_REFUSED` → Chen in epilogue: "That was Elise's failsafe. You couldn't have known. But thank you."

### Vault 49 terminal puzzle (Ch.2)

A three-document reading sequence recovered from a Cold War bunker's terminals: Chen's resignation memo (2021), Elena Ortega's dissent transcript from the final advisory board vote (2022), the vote tally itself. Completing the sequence sets `VAULT49_TERMINALS_READ`. This flag causes Elise Voss to invoke Elena Ortega by name in Phase 2 of the final fight — the single strongest rebuttal in the game.

### Tilly's father recording (Vault 49, optional Ch.2/3 backtrack)

If the Tilly bond arc (Ch.1 campfire + Ch.3 speaking scene) is completed, a previously-inactive recording in Vault 49 becomes available on backtrack. It is a whistleblower confession from an SI Inc. junior engineer — Tilly's father — recorded the night he left to meet a journalist who then buried the story. Sets `TILLY_FATHER_HEARD`. No mechanical effect, only an epilogue line.

### Elise's daughter (Ch.5 final boss, Phase 3)

An audio log plays during Phase 3 of the Elise fight — a voicemail from Elise's daughter Nora, dated 2014, the day before she was killed when a distracted driver ran a red light. The log does not absolve Elise. It does answer the question: "Why this, why you." The log plays regardless of talk-down route. If the player chose talk-down, the final player response references Nora.

### Warden Six (recurring mini-boss)

See `side_characters.md` for full arc. Appears in Ch.1 Harlan Mine exit, Ch.3 Storm Corridor, and a secret Ch.5 Silicon Valley maintenance-corridor encounter (gated by `SIX_BEATEN_CH1 && SIX_BEATEN_CH3`). The Ch.5 drop — `Six's Core` — enables a single-use combo ability usable only in the Elise fight.

### Mr. Gray (Ch.5 Boardroom antechamber)

Rehearsal for the Elise fight. An aging SI Inc. VP who presents Elise's arguments in a weaker form. Talk-down option unlocks if `convertedCured > convertedFought`. See `dialogue_choices.md` §5.

### Lila Chen (Ch.5 Campus Perimeter)

Dr. Chen's adult converted daughter. Encountered during Chen's solo stealth route. Three-option choice (Cure / Fight / Leave). Chen has three distinct epilogue lines, one per branch. See `dialogue_choices.md` §4.
